Project Overview

BAJA SAE is one of the world's premier student engineering competitions, requiring teams to design, build, and deploy full-scale off-road vehicles. As team captain, I lead a multidisciplinary team of 42 engineers through every phase of vehicle development, from initial concept through competition performance.

Key Responsibilities

  • Team Leadership: Captain and strategic lead for 42-person engineering team spanning multiple disciplines
  • CAD Design: Oversee and manage complete vehicle CAD modeling using professional design tools
  • Structural Analysis: Coordinate CAE (Finite Element Analysis) for chassis and critical components
  • Manufacturing Management: Direct CAM operations and coordinate manufacturing across all subsystems
  • System Integration: Ensure seamless integration between mechanical, drivetrain, suspension, and electrical systems
  • Field Testing & Validation: Oversee testing protocols and vehicle validation before competition deployment

Technical Implementation

The vehicle design integrates advanced CAE simulations for structural integrity, suspension dynamics, and powertrain optimization. CAM processes ensure precision manufacturing of critical components. The ongoing project maintains and continuously improves the vehicle based on competition performance data and field testing feedback. From 2019 to present, the team has iterated through multiple generations of the BAJA vehicle, each incorporating lessons learned and performance enhancements.
